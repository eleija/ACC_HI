About


About


The Clarke Center brings together world class researchers in science, art, humanities, engineering and medicine to work on a new theoretical framework for exploring the basis of imagination. Through a global network of scientific and cultural partners we transfer discoveries from the University to the world beyond.


Our Goals


Research at the neural, behavioral and social level into how imagination occurs; develop more effective ways of using imagination in education and learning; and enhance the appreciation of imagination's role in confronting humanity’s problems.


What we do


The center sponsors seed grants, assembles workshops and conferences, and provides the administrative and advisory support to faculty and affiliates to write grants on imagination related topics. See our RESEARCH page for an account of some of current research themes and a list of projects underway among center faculty.

Another one of our major interests is to stimulate new styles and forms of science fiction writing. The Clarion workshop is an intensive six-week summer program sponsored by the Clarion Foundation and hosted at UCSD each year, in which select students have an opportunity to interact with eminent scientists and noted science fiction writers to hone their skills in writing science fiction and fantasy short stories.


Support our Vision


The Clarke Center was formed through a competitive award from the Arthur C Clarke Foundation in 2012. The center's growth depends on research grants, alliances, strategic partnerships and the support of an interested public. Support can take many forms: from suggestions for research topics, proposals for collaboration on research or conferences to contributions.

External Board

The function of the External Advisory Board of the Clarke Center is to ensure that our vision remains international, experienced and far-reaching. Members come from a broad range of sectors and provide advice on our research agenda, on our strategies for public engagement and on directions for growth. The advisory board meets annually and all members are volunteers.

  • Sir Ken Robinson - International advisor on education in the arts
  • Kim Stanley Robinson - Science Fiction Author
  • Larry Smarr - Institute Director of Calit2
  • Ian Goldin - Director, Oxford Martin School
  • Gretchen Kalonji - Assistant Director-General for Natural Sciences
  • Pete Worden - NASA AMES director
  • Mark Dankberg - CEO ViaSat
  • Tedson Meyers - Chairman, Clarke Foundation Board
  • Peter Diamandis - Entrepreneur, Co-Founder of Planetary Resources, Inc., Chairman of the X PRIZE Foundation
